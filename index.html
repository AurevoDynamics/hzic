Solvation-Engineered Hybrid Zinc-Ion Capacitors

Polymer gel electrolytes · EDLC buffering · Controlled zinc electrochemistry

The Zinc Problem

Conventional aqueous zinc systems fail under high power due to dendritic growth, hydrogen evolution, and uncontrolled ion flux.

Our Approach

Hybrid Architecture

Zinc battery chemistry integrated with an EDLC branch, electronically decoupled via active DC–DC control.

Solvation-Regulated Gel Electrolyte

PVA-based polymer gels engineered to restructure Zn²⁺ solvation, homogenize ion flux, and suppress dendrite initiation.

Active Current Partitioning

EDLC absorbs transient power demands while zinc operates strictly within electrochemically safe CC–CV limits.

System Architecture

The HZIC system integrates electrochemical, polymer, and power-electronic constraints into a unified architecture optimized for safety and power.
